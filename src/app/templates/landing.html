Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about the PRIS Act

What is the PRIS Act?

The Privacy and Responsible Information Sharing (PRIS) Act is Western Australia's comprehensive privacy legislation that regulates how organizations collect, use, disclose, and store personal information. It establishes 11 Information Privacy Principles (IPPs) that organizations must follow to protect individuals' privacy rights.

Who needs to comply?

The PRIS Act applies to Community Service Providers (CSPs) in Western Australia, including state government agencies, local governments, universities, and organizations that receive government funding to provide community services. If your organization handles personal information as a CSP, you need to comply with the PRIS Act.

What happens if I don't comply?

Non-compliance with the PRIS Act can result in serious consequences including regulatory investigations, mandatory compliance orders, reputational damage, and potential penalties. Organizations may face complaints to the Information Commissioner, and breaches can lead to enforcement actions. Mandatory breach notification requirements (effective January 2027) mean failures to report can result in additional penalties.

When does it take effect?

The PRIS Act comes into full effect in July 2026. Organizations should begin compliance preparations now to ensure they have adequate systems, policies, and procedures in place. Key requirements include designating a Privacy Officer, conducting Privacy Impact Assessments, maintaining a register of personal information holdings, and establishing processes for handling access and correction requests.
